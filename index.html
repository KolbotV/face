Building a Skills-Based Career Preparation Pathway through Community Engagement for Arts and Humanities Students

Exploring mutually beneficial methods for facilitating and recognizing student learning in with community partners in Minnesota.

About the project

This project will seek to create opportunities for faculty, staff, and students at three Minnesota liberal arts colleges to connect with community consultants and collaborators in order to gather ideas about the feasibility of designing a skills-based career preparation pathway program for arts and humanities students that will be mutually beneficial for all participants. Project leads and student collaborators will gather tools from guest artists, storytellers, and speakers who use arts and humanities approaches to facilitate interactions around the project theme of "Belonging."

Building Career Skills Through Arts & Community

This Project is funded by a Faculty Career Enhancement (FaCE) Grant from the Associated Colleges of the Midwest (ACM)
